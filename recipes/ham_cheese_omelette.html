The Omelette

Omelettes are delicious and nutritious for several reasons. They are made from eggs, which are a great source of protein, essential for building and repairing tissues in the body, as well as providing energy. Eggs are also rich in vitamins and minerals such as vitamin D, vitamin B12, and selenium, which are important for maintaining good health. Additionally, omelettes are often filled with a variety of vegetables, such as spinach, tomatoes, onions, and mushrooms, which add flavor, texture, and additional nutrients like fiber, vitamins, and minerals to the dish. Vegetables are also low in calories and high in antioxidants, making them an excellent addition to a healthy diet. Finally, omelettes are a versatile dish that can be customized to suit individual preferences, whether you prefer a classic cheese omelette or one filled with a variety of vegetables and meats, an omelette can be a satisfying and nutritious meal any time of day.

Ingredients

  • Three Eggs
  • Sliced Ham
  • Mexican Cheese
  • Herbs de Provence

These ingredients are what you will need for this recipe. Although there are many different types of omelettes, today we will be making a ham and cheese omelette.

Lets do this!

  1. Crack two eggs into a bowl and whisk them with a fork.
  2. Heat a non-stick frying pan over medium-high heat.
  3. Add a small amount of butter or oil to the pan and let it melt.
  4. Pour the whisked eggs into the pan and let them cook for about 1-2 minutes, until the bottom starts to set.
  5. Using a spatula, gently lift the edges of the omelette and let the uncooked egg flow underneath to cook.
  6. Once the omelette is mostly cooked, add a handful of shredded cheese and some chopped ham to one half of the omelette.
  7. Use the spatula to carefully fold the other half of the omelette over the filling.
  8. Let the omelette cook for another 30 seconds to 1 minute, until the cheese is melted and the filling is heated through.
  9. Slide the omelette onto a plate and serve hot.
